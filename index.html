The Malaysian Bioscience Scenario

Harumanis mangoes (Mangifera indica cv. 'Harumanis') are Perlis's premium agrotech export. Under automated greenhouse/fertigation controls, tracking physical growth helps prevent fruit splitting and nutrient waste.

By modeling cumulative fruit mass $f(t)$ using a sigmoidal logistic equation, we find the instantaneous growth rate $f'(t)$ (grams per day). This rate indicates exactly when cell expansion is fastest, requiring intense irrigation, and when growth ceases, requiring dry conditions to lock in sweetness.

Realistic Malaysian Standards & Datasets:
  • MARDI: Manual Penanaman Mangga Harumanis (benchmark weight ~450g over 120 days post-anthesis).
  • Jabatan Pertanian Malaysia: Harumanis Perlis Standard Code of Practice.

Mathematical Model

Growth Function (Logistic Curve)
$$f(t) = \frac{W_{max}}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$
First Derivative (Rate of Mass Gain)
$$f'(t) = \frac{W_{max} \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t - t_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)})^2}$$

Alternatively, using the logistic differentiation identity: $f'(t) = k \cdot f(t) \cdot \left(1 - \frac{f(t)}{W_{max}}\right)$.

Parameter Configurations:
Max Weight ($W_{max}$) 450 g
Growth Coeff ($k$) 0.08
Mid-point ($t_0$) Day 60

What is $f'(t)$ telling us?

In calculus, a derivative represents the instantaneous rate of change. In your Agrotech degree, $f(t)$ tells you how heavy the mango is today, whereas $f'(t)$ tells you how many grams of cells/water the mango is actively building right now, per day. If $f'(t)$ is low, nutrients are redirecting elsewhere; if it is high, the physiological sink is at maximum tension.
